PATENT / UM / ID APPLICATION:

    Patent / Utility Model / Industrial Design Application — Step-by-Step
    Application Requirements:
    1. Start
    2. Initial submission to ITSSO (for evaluation) – Client
    • Prior Art Search Report
    • Invention Disclosure Form
    • Endorsement Letter
    • Declaration of Co-Authorship
    3. ITSSO/IPC review & evaluation – ITSSO/IPC

    4. Decision point
    • If NOT patentable/registrable: process ends.
    • If patentable/registrable: Notice of Application is issued → proceed.

    5. Submit application requirements – Client (with ITSSO support)
    • Patent Documents*
    • Application Form *
    • Copy of Valid ID*
    • Special Power of Attorney (if applicable)*

    6. File the application with IPOPHL – ITSSO/Client

    7. (Optional) Request for Issuance of Special Order – ITSSO
    Used when needed to facilitate processing.

    8. Processing paths at IPOPHL
    A. For Utility Model / Industrial Design
    • Formality Examination
    • Publication of UM / Industrial Design Application
    B. For Patent
    • Publication for Opposition
    • Substantive Examination

    9. Registration outcome – IPOPHL
    • Issuance of Certificate of Registration & Publication of registration

    10. Incentives & closing – Client (with ITSSO support) • Request for Incentive • Claim incentive and the Certificate of Registration (inventor) • End

Trade Mark Application

Trademark Application — Step-by-Step
Application Requirements:
1. Start
2. Initial submission to ITSSO (for evaluation) – Client
• IP Assitance Request Form
• Trademark Documents
• Soft Copy of Registrable Mark
• Endorsement Letter
3. Review & evaluation of the application – ITSSO

4. Decision point
• If NOT registrable: process ends.
• If registrable: Notice of Application is issued → proceed.

5. Submit application requirements – Client (with ITSSO support)
• Application Form*
• Copy of Valid ID*
• Special Power of Attorney (if applicable)*

6. File the application with IPOPHL – ITSSO/Client

7. (Optional) Request for Issuance of Special Order – ITSSO
Used when needed to facilitate processing.

8. Processing at IPOPHL
• Formality & Substantive Examination
• Publication for Opposition

9. Registration outcome – IPOPHL
• Issuance of Certificate of Registration
• Publication of Registered Trademark

10. Closing – Client (with ITSSO support)
• Release of Certificate of Registration to trademark holder
• Maintenance for Declaration of Actual Use (DAU) or Renewal
• End

COPYRIGHT APPLICATION

Copyright Application — Step-by-Step
Application Requirements:
1. Start

2. Initial submission to ITSSO (for evaluation) – Client
• IP Assistance Request Form
• Copyright Form
• Endorsement Letter
• 1 Soft Copy of the Work

3. Review & evaluation of the documents – ITSSO

4. Decision point
• If NOT registrable: process ends.
• If registrable: Notice of Application is issued → proceed.

5. Submit application requirements – Client (with ITSSO support)
• Application Form*
• Deed of Assignment* (if applicable)
• Copy of Valid ID*
• Certificate of Language Editing*
• Certificate of Originality Examination*

6. File the application with IPOPHL / National Library of the Philippines – ITSSO/Client

7. Processing at IPOPHL / NLP
• Formality check
• Evaluation and approval

8. Registration outcome – IPOPHL / NLP
• Issuance of Certificate of Registration

9. Closing – Client (with ITSSO support)
• Release of Certificate of Registration to creator/s or author/s
• End

You may contact us for more information about this Process.

About ITSSO

The SLSU Innovation & Technology Support Services Office (ITSSO), which is formerly known as Program/Project Planning and Administrative Support (PPAS) Services, is a frontline service unit of the Office of the Vice President for Research, Extension, Production, Development, and Innovation (VP-REPDI). Its main function is to enhance the service delivery capacity of the Research, Extension, and Production through innovation, technology utilization, consultancy services towards commercialization, and ethical review services.


Goals

The goal of the SLSU Innovation & Technology Support Services Office (ITSSO) is to strengthen the university’s research, extension, and production endeavors by promoting innovation, facilitating technology transfer and commercialization, providing consultancy and ethical review services, and assisting innovators in securing intellectual property rights such as patents and utility models.


Objectives

1. To provide students the opportunities to become mature and responsible persons with the values embodied in the college’s mission statement as evident in their innovative and active responses psychologically, spiritually and develop environmentally sound and market-driven technologies at par with the international standards.
2. To instill relevant and responsive academic programs and professional trainings that will enhance graduates for national development.
3. To prepare and train the students for appropriate competency standards by providing relevant programs, competent faculty, modern facilities, expand linkages for productivity enhancement and community empowerment.
4. To develop students as leaders, practitioners and to become globally competitive individuals.
